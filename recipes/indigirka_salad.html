Indigirka Salad

Indigirka salad is a traditional Russian salad that is named after the Indigirka River in the northeastern part of Siberia. It is a hearty and filling salad that is perfect for the cold winter months.

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3-4 medium-sized carrots, peeled and cubed
  • 1-2 small beets, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup canned or cooked peas
  • 1/2 cup canned or cooked corn
  • 1/2 cup diced pickles
  • 1/2 cup diced cooked ham or bologna
  • 1/2 cup diced cooked chicken or turkey
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Steps:

  1. Place the potatoes, carrots, and beets in a pot and cover them with water. Bring the water to a boil and then reduce the heat to medium-low. Simmer the vegetables for 10-15 minutes, or until they are fork-tender.
  2. Drain the vegetables and let them cool to room temperature.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooled vegetables with the peas, corn, pickles, ham or bologna, and chicken or turkey.
  4. Add mayonnaise to the bowl and mix well to combine. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the salad for at least an hour, or overnight.
  6. Before serving, garnish the indigirka salad with fresh parsley.
